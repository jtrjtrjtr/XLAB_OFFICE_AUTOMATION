# Monitoring the event tech and immersive experience landscape: a curated source catalog for XLAB

**XLAB can build a world-class trend knowledge base by systematically monitoring roughly 50 high-quality sources across eight categories.** The event production and experiential technology ecosystem is served by a surprisingly rich — if fragmented — media landscape, with strong publications in the US (Event Marketer, BizBash) and globally (Blooloop, AVIXA), but a notable gap in dedicated CEE-region coverage. This catalog maps over 200 verified sources organized by XLAB's four strategic tracks, rated for quality and relevance, with actionable recommendations for immediate monitoring. The most efficient approach combines five meta-sources that aggregate content across the industry with targeted deep-dive sources for each track.

## C) Award programs and competitions

Award archives are the single most valuable source of documented case studies. The best programs function as searchable inspiration databases spanning years of winning projects.

### Tier 1 — Essential inspiration databases (★★★★★)

**Prix Ars Electronica** (ars.electronica.art/prix/) is the crown jewel: a searchable archive spanning **1987–2025** with detailed descriptions, photos, videos, and jury statements for every entry. Free to browse. Categories include Interactive Art+, Artificial Life & Intelligence, and Digital Communities. Nearly **4,000 submissions from 98 countries** annually. Most relevant for Tracks 3 and 4.

**Cannes Lions — Brand Experience & Activation** (lovethework.com) provides the gold standard for experiential marketing case studies. Subcategories cover Excellence in Brand Experience, Retail Experience, Touchpoints & Technology, and Social Engagement for Live Experience. The Work archive has full case study videos and detailed write-ups. Subscription required for full access. Most relevant for Track 2.

**D&AD Awards** (dandad.org) maintains a searchable archive back to **1962** with Spatial Design (exhibitions, pop-ups, installations, stages) and Experiential (Events/Stunts, Live Experiences, Digital Activations, Use of Technology/XR) categories. Well-organized filtering by year, category, and award level. Most relevant for Tracks 2 and 3.

## D) Festivals, conferences, and trade fairs

Industry events serve dual purposes: networking and direct inspiration. The most valuable events also publish extensive post-event content, extending their value year-round.

### Must-attend tier (★★★★★)

**ISE (Integrated Systems Europe)** in Barcelona (February) is the cornerstone AV event: **85,000 attendees, 1,600 exhibitors** across all AV disciplines. Covers every track. Free expo passes with early registration. YouTube channel publishes session recordings. XLAB's most important annual trade fair.

**Ars Electronica Festival** in Linz (September) draws **122,000+ visits** with 379 exhibits from 1,472 artists/scientists across 83 countries. Only **~2 hours from Prague** — the closest world-class art-tech festival to XLAB's home base. Extensive free YouTube archives and downloadable PDFs. Covers Tracks 3 and 4.

**SXSW** in Austin (March) is unmatched for cross-disciplinary trend-spotting with **161,000+ attendees** across tech, music, and film. The brand activation showcase is massive. Publishes **hundreds of session recordings for free** on YouTube — the most generous post-event content library of any major event.

**C2 Montréal** (May) is itself a case study in experiential design — founded by Sid Lee and Cirque du Soleil, named "Best Conference" by BizBash four years running. **5,000–6,500 attendees** experience the conference as an immersive installation. Covers all four tracks.

**SIGGRAPH** in Los Angeles (July) is the global standard for **real-time graphics, immersive tech, and visual computing** with 15,000–20,000 attendees. The Real-Time Live!, Immersive Pavilion, and Emerging Technologies programs directly serve XLAB's technical staff. Massive free content archive on YouTube and ACM Digital Library.

### Worth monitoring tier (★★★–★★★½)

PLASA Show (London, September — free, fills gap left by **Prolight+Sound Frankfurt's cancellation** in late 2025), InfoComm (Las Vegas, June), Event Tech Live (London, November — free), IMEX Frankfurt (May — Europe's largest MICE show), MuseumNext (rotating European cities), Noor Riyadh (November — city-scale, extremely well-funded by Saudi cultural investment), and LLUM BCN (Barcelona, February — convenient timing near ISE).

**Critical update:** Prolight+Sound Frankfurt has been permanently cancelled. PLASA London and ISE Barcelona are now the primary European entertainment tech trade shows.

### Events with the best free post-event content

SXSW, SIGGRAPH, and Ars Electronica publish hundreds of session recordings for free on YouTube. Web Summit, Adobe MAX, and Slush also publish extensively. ISE and AVIXA provide partial free content. Most light festivals and C2 Montréal require in-person attendance — content is not captured for later viewing.

## Identified gaps in the source landscape

**Dedicated CEE coverage is absent.** No publication, newsletter, or award program specifically covers the Central and Eastern European event production or creative technology scene. XLAB could fill this gap through thought leadership content.

**Track 4 (AI/digital humans at events) has the weakest coverage.** While AI is discussed broadly, specific case studies of AI avatars, conversational kiosks, and AI-personalized experiences at live events are scarce. Most coverage is about the technology itself, not its event applications. The field is moving faster than the media can document.

**Technical depth is inversely correlated with creative coverage.** AV trade press (AVNetwork, AV Magazine) explains how technology works but rarely covers the creative vision. Creative publications (Event Marketer, The Drum) cover the what and why but skip technical details. Only a handful of sources — **Blooloop, CreativeApplications.Net, and disguise's case studies** — consistently bridge both.

**Middle East event coverage is thin despite massive investment.** Saudi Arabia (Noor Riyadh, NEOM, Entertainment Authority) and UAE (Expo City Dubai, Museum of the Future) are pouring billions into event technology, but English-language documentation of specific projects and case studies remains limited.

**Post-event content from European events is poor compared to US events.** SXSW publishes hundreds of free recordings; ISE publishes highlights. C2 Montréal, BOE International, and most European light festivals publish almost nothing. This means attending European events in person remains irreplaceable.

**Robotics in show production** — mentioned in Track 4 — has virtually no dedicated coverage. Individual projects (Kuka robot arms at events, Boston Dynamics performances) appear sporadically but no source systematically tracks this niche.

## Recommended monitoring cadence

For a team of one person spending **30 minutes daily**, the optimal routine combines three daily newsletters (Event Marketer, Blooloop, BizBash) with weekly scans of Immersive Wire and CreativeApplications.Net, monthly reviews of award winner archives (rotating through Ars Electronica, Cannes Lions, D&AD), and quarterly reads of Freeman XLNC and Skift Megatrends reports. Add annual attendance at ISE (February), Ars Electronica (September), and Signal Festival (October) for the in-person inspiration that no publication can replace.
